## Description SEO

**Optimal structure (top to bottom):**
1. First 2-3 lines (above the fold): primary keyword in first 25 words
2. Body (200+ chars): natural prose with keywords, 300-350 words target
3. Timestamps: `0:00 - Section Name` with keyword-rich labels
4. Links and CTAs: related videos, playlists, social profiles
5. Hashtags: 3-5 at the very end (never in first lines)

Long-tail keywords outperform broad terms. "Obsidian daily notes for developers"
beats "productivity". 18% of top-10 search results come from channels < 1K subs.

## Thumbnail & CTR

- Custom face + power-word text: 6-12% CTR vs auto-generated 1-2%
- 1 idea, 3 elements max, 0-4 words
- High contrast: dark background + bright accent color
- Face with emotion (surprise/excitement) occupies 40-60%
- Brand consistency: same colors, font, layout across all videos

## Self-Review Framework

### Metrics (priority order for small channels)

| Metric | Target | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Audience retention (avg %) | 45%+ for 5-10 min | Content holds interest |
| CTR | 4-10% | Packaging attracts clicks |
| Watch time (absolute) | Cumulative hours | Algorithmic ranking |
| Comments (quality) | Questions/opinions | Genuine engagement |

### Video Self-Review Checklist
- [ ] Hook delivers on thumbnail promise within 15 seconds
- [ ] Strongest value front-loaded (first 30 seconds)
- [ ] Pacing change every 20-30 seconds (cut, screen switch, zoom)
- [ ] Outro has no dead air — CTA before engagement drops
- [ ] Subscription CTA placed BEFORE average watch duration ends
- [ ] Subscription CTA anchored to value-release moment

### Retention Benchmarks
- 1-3 min: 60%+ good, 75%+ excellent
- 5-10 min: 45%+ good, 60%+ excellent
- 10-20 min: 40%+ good, 55%+ excellent

## English (ESL) Feedback — CEFR Levels

Assess spoken English based on CEFR framework (tracktest.eu/english-levels-cefr).

### CEFR Level Descriptors (for video assessment)

| Level | Speaking Characteristics |
|---|---|
| A1 | Basic phrases, isolated words, cannot sustain |
| A2 | Simple sentences, familiar topics, frequent pauses |
| B1 | Can sustain on familiar topics, limited range, noticeable L1 transfer |
| B2 | Clear, detailed, can argue pros/cons, occasional errors |
| C1 | Fluent, spontaneous, flexible, rare errors |
| C2 | Effortless, precise, nuanced even in complex topics |

### Assessment Criteria
1. **Grammar accuracy**: articles, subject-verb agreement, tense consistency
2. **Vocabulary range**: technical vs general, variety of connectors
3. **Fluency**: hesitation markers, self-correction, speech rate
4. **Coherence**: logical structure, topic transitions, discourse markers
5. **Pronunciation indicators**: from transcript artifacts (repeated words = pronunciation difficulty)

### Common Korean→English Transfer Issues
- Article omission: "I opened terminal" → "I opened **the** terminal"
- Subject-verb agreement: "it work" → "it **works**"
- Filler repetition: "so so", "I I" = hesitation, not error
- End-of-sentence trailing: "so, yeah" → replace with clean transition

### Feedback Template
```
### English (CEFR Assessment)
**Level**: B1후반 / B2초반 (estimated from this video)
**잘한 점**:
- [specific examples of good usage from transcript]
**개선 포인트**:
- [specific grammar/vocab issues with corrections]
**Youglish 추천**: [words to practice pronunciation]
```

## Tech/Developer Niche (Obsidian/AI/Coding)

- Niche specificity > breadth (3x faster growth)
- Project-based > concept-based (viewers want artifacts)
- Optimal length: 8-15 min for tutorials
- Screen recording: large font, zoom on CLI, narrate window switches
- Content cadence (0-100 subs): 1-2 long-form/week
- First 6 months: cover core niche exclusively

## 15-Element Growth Checklist (주언규PD)

1. Thumbnail/Title (CTR hook)
2. Opening intro (value delivery in 15s)
3. Script structure (conclusion → evidence → examples)
4. Audio quality (louder than visuals)
5. Editing for clarity (pacing changes)
6. Consistent character/persona
7. Category focus (single niche)
8. Style uniformity
9. Item/tool selection
10. Likability (1+ honest limitation per video)
11. Relatability (specific situations, not generalizations)
12. Usefulness (concrete takeaways)
13. Trend timing
14. Narrative structure (goal → obstacle → choice → result → lesson)
15. External traffic (collabs, community)

**Key insight**: These are multiplicative, not additive. One zero factor kills growth.
